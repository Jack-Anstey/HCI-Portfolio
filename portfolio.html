Human Computer Interaction: A Design Portfolio

Individual Projects

The first half of my very first assignment. Tasked with finding and explaining a piece of bad design that Emory hosts, I chose to illuminate the distaster that is Emory Opus.
The second half of my very first assignment. Emory does host webpages with good design, so I chose to focus on the resurrection of Course Atlas, which has improved greatly during my time at Emory.
Our second individual assignment, which focused on Needfinding in airports. Being a college student without a car, I asked other college students around the United States how they got to and from the airport, and which ways they would rather to get to the airport if they were given the choice.
Following my work in Needfinding, I designed Bag.Drop: a service to help those get to and from the airport with numerous bags with efficiency and ease.
Accessibility is a massive issue. Accessible design benefits everyone, not just those who directly need it. With that in mind, how accessible is Emory's campus? As I found out, there's much work to be done to make campus an accessible place for everyone.
